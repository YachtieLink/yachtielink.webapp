# YachtieLink — 5-Year Path to €10M ARR

> **Status:** Brainstorming. Not canonical. Does not override any docs in `/docs/`.

---

## The Core Insight

Crew Pro at €12/month can't get there alone. 10,000 paying crew = €1.44M ARR. The superyacht crew pool is ~50–100K people globally. Even with extraordinary penetration, crew subscriptions cap around €2M ARR.

The path to €10M is: **build the trust graph with crew, then sell verification access to the demand side.** The graph is the product. Crew build it for free. Everyone else pays to read it.

This doesn't break the constitutional rules — trust creation stays free and uninfluenced. Payment buys *access* to verified information, not *influence* over it.

## Year-by-Year Plan

### Year 1 (2026): Prove the Wedge

**Build target:** Phase 1A (what's already specced)

**Milestones:**
- Ship MVP by June (Med season start)
- 2,000 crew profiles by December
- 500 yacht entities with real attachments
- First endorsements flowing organically
- Crew Pro live with Stripe — target 100 paying users by EOY

**Revenue:** €1–6K MRR

**Geography:** Antibes, Palma, Fort Lauderdale — the three crew hubs. In-person seeding at crew agencies, bars, boat shows. Not scalable. Doesn't need to be.

**Critical validation:**
- Does the yacht graph loop actually work? (Crew joins → attaches yacht → sees colleagues → requests endorsement → colleague joins to respond)
- What's the time-to-first-endorsement? Target: <21 days
- Do crew voluntarily share their profile link? (organic share rate >10%)

**What NOT to do:** Don't chase user counts. Don't open recruiter access. Don't expand beyond superyachts. The graph must be dense before it's wide.

---

### Year 2 (2027): Density Before Distribution

**Build target:** Phase 1B + start Phase 1C

**Milestones:**
- 10,000–15,000 crew profiles
- 3,000+ yachts with established status
- Graph density: average crew member has 5+ colleagues on platform
- Endorsement completion rate >40%
- Launch recruiter access (Phase 1C) in Q3

**New revenue lines:**
- **Recruiter seats** (€79/mo) — search crew who've opted into discovery, filter by yacht size, role, certifications, endorsement count. Sorting by endorsements is allowed (D-026) but recruiter never influences trust creation.
- **Agency plans** (€299/mo) — multi-seat, shortlisting, candidate pipelines, bulk search. Same rules: access to the graph, never influence over it.

**Revenue:** €20–50K MRR

**Key moves:**
- Availability toggle with 7-day expiry (already specced in Phase 1B)
- Limited crew search for Pro users (already specced)
- Recruiter dashboard — separate product surface, separate pricing
- Contextual profile visibility: direct link = full profile, search result = locked summary (D-025). Crew controls what recruiters see.

**Geography:** Expand to Caribbean, Middle East (Dubai), Australia/NZ. Still crew-hub focused.

**Constitutional checkpoint:** Recruiter revenue must never create pressure to weaken crew privacy controls. The moment you optimise for recruiter conversion at the expense of crew trust, you've lost the moat.

### Year 3 (2028): Become the Verification Standard

**This is the inflection year.** The graph is now dense enough that "not on YachtieLink" starts to mean something.

**Build target:** Verification API + management company tools

**Milestones:**
- 25,000–40,000 crew profiles
- 6,000+ yachts
- First enterprise API contracts signed
- First insurance partnership
- "Verified on YachtieLink" becomes a phrase people use

**New revenue lines:**

**Verification API** — the big unlock. Third parties can query the graph:
- "Does this person have verified employment on MY/Serenity between 2024–2026?" → Yes/No + endorsement count
- "How many colleagues have endorsed this crew member?" → Count (not content)
- Crew must consent to each verification query (consent layer is critical)
- API pricing: per-query (€0.50–2.00) or monthly subscription (€2–10K/mo)

**Who buys the API:**
- **Yacht management companies** (Hill Robinson, Burgess, Camper & Nicholsons) — verify crew before placement. Currently done by phone calls and PDFs. YachtieLink replaces a manual, error-prone process.
- **Insurance underwriters** — crew competency affects hull/P&I premiums. Verified crew history reduces underwriting risk. This is real money.
- **Charter companies** — need to verify crew on charter yachts for guest safety and liability.
- **Flag states / MCA** — potential long-term, but regulatory adoption is slow.

**Management company tools** (€499–999/mo):
- Fleet-level crew verification dashboard
- Compliance tracking (cert expiry, sea time)
- Crew rotation planning with graph data
- Not a hiring tool — a verification and compliance tool

**Revenue:** €100–200K MRR

**Geography:** Global superyacht coverage. The API doesn't care about geography.

**Critical move:** Sign 2–3 anchor management companies as design partners in Q1. Build the API with them, not for them. Their workflows define the product surface.

### Year 4 (2029): Enterprise Expansion

**Build target:** Deepen enterprise contracts, expand to adjacent maritime

**Milestones:**
- 50,000+ crew profiles
- Verification API embedded in 3+ management company platforms
- First insurance product tied to YachtieLink verification
- Expand crew definition beyond superyachts: sailing yachts, charter fleets, expedition vessels

**New revenue lines:**

**Insurance products:**
- Partner with P&I clubs and hull insurers
- "YachtieLink-verified crew" = reduced premium tier
- Revenue share on premium reduction or flat API fee
- This is where serious money starts flowing — insurance is a €2B+ market in yachting alone

**Training academy integrations** (€300–800/mo):
- Academies push cert completions directly to crew profiles
- Eliminates fake certificates (a real problem in yachting)
- Crew benefit: certs auto-verified, no manual upload
- Academy benefit: their graduates are more discoverable

**Expanded crew market:**
- Sailing yacht crew (~30,000 globally)
- Charter fleet crew (~20,000)
- Expedition/research vessel crew (~10,000)
- Same trust model, same graph mechanics, different yacht types

**Revenue:** €350–500K MRR

**Constitutional checkpoint:** As enterprise revenue grows, the pressure to give enterprise customers influence over the graph will intensify. This is where "freeze before corruption" gets tested. The API must remain read-only on trust data. No enterprise customer can write to the graph.

---

### Year 5 (2030): Industry Infrastructure

**Build target:** Standard of record

**Milestones:**
- 80,000–100,000 crew profiles
- Verification API is industry standard
- Insurance underwriting requires or incentivises YachtieLink verification
- Management companies can't operate without it (switching cost is graph density)
- €800K–1M MRR → **€10M ARR**

**The endgame position:** YachtieLink is to yachting crew what a credit score is to lending — not a product you choose to use, but infrastructure the industry runs on. Except unlike credit scores, the crew owns their data and controls access.

**Revenue mix stabilises:**
- ~12% crew subscriptions
- ~17% recruiter/agency access
- ~11% management company tools
- ~52% verification API + insurance + enterprise
- ~8% training integrations

**Moat:** The colleague graph is almost impossible to replicate. Every crew member who joins adds edges to dozens of colleagues across multiple yachts. A competitor would need to rebuild years of employment history and trust signals. By Year 5, the graph contains the working history of the industry.

## The Four Inflection Points

| # | Threshold | When | What Unlocks |
|---|-----------|------|-------------|
| 1 | **2,000 crew, 500 yachts** | End Y1 | Organic endorsement loop — crew pull in colleagues without prompting |
| 2 | **10,000 crew, 3,000 yachts** | Mid Y2 | Recruiter demand — enough coverage that agencies can't ignore the platform |
| 3 | **30,000 crew** | Mid Y3 | Verification standard — "not on YachtieLink" becomes a signal |
| 4 | **60,000+ crew** | Y4–Y5 | Industry infrastructure — embedded in enterprise workflows, switching cost is prohibitive |

Each threshold gates the next revenue layer. You can't sell recruiter access before threshold 2. You can't sell the API before threshold 3. Trying to skip ahead breaks the growth loop.

## What This Means for the Build

**Phase 1A (now):** Exactly what's specced. Don't change anything. Prove the wedge.

**Phase 1B (late 2026):** Availability toggle, limited search. Prep for recruiter access.

**Phase 1C (2027):** Recruiter access, agency plans. First demand-side revenue.

**Phase 2 (2028):** Verification API, management company tools. The business model shift — from consumer subscriptions to enterprise infrastructure.

**Phase 3 (2029–2030):** Insurance partnerships, training integrations, market expansion. Scale the infrastructure play.

The constitutional rules don't just protect crew — they protect the business. The moment trust is purchasable, the graph's value to enterprise customers collapses. The API is only worth paying for because the data is incorruptible. **Your principles are your pricing power.**

---

## Risks That Kill This

1. **Graph doesn't loop** (Y1) — If crew don't pull in colleagues, you have profiles without a graph. The product is LinkedIn with a boat emoji. Dead.
2. **Recruiter revenue corrupts incentives** (Y2) — If you start optimising for recruiter conversion, you'll weaken crew controls. The graph degrades. Enterprise won't trust it later.
3. **Enterprise customers demand write access** (Y3–4) — "Let us flag crew" or "let us weight endorsements." If you bend, you're a management company's HR tool, not an industry standard.
4. **Regulatory capture** (Y4–5) — If a flag state mandates a competitor or builds their own, you lose the infrastructure play. Mitigation: be embedded before regulation catches up.
5. **Crew churn** (ongoing) — Yachting is transient. People leave the industry. If departing crew delete profiles, the graph loses edges. Mitigation: make the profile useful beyond active job seeking (alumni network, reference archive).

## One-Line Summary

Build the trust graph with crew for free. Sell read access to everyone who needs to verify crew. Never sell write access to anyone. That's a €10M business.
