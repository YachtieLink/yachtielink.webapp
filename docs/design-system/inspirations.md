# Inspirations

Reference points for how YachtieLink should look and feel. When you're making a design choice, check these to calibrate. Add new inspirations as they come up.

---

## Primary Inspirations

### Notion (notion.so)
**What to take:** Visual energy, personality through colour and illustration, bento grid layouts, mascot character (Nosey), bold section colours, purposeful animation, the feeling that a productivity tool can still be *fun*.

**Specifically:**
- Colour-coded feature sections (each area has its own accent)
- Mascot appears in empty states and celebrations, never in-your-face
- Mixed card sizes create visual rhythm
- Generous whitespace despite dense information
- Animation on entrance and state change, not on everything

**What NOT to take:** Complexity. Notion is a power tool. YachtieLink is a simple profile — five tabs, one purpose.

### Bumble
**What to take:** Photo-forward profiles, swipeable photo galleries, card-based layout, the balance between "dating app energy" and "respectful adult experience."

**Specifically:**
- Full-bleed hero photo (65vh on mobile)
- Dot indicators for photo count
- Split layout on desktop (photo left, content right)
- Cards with rounded corners and soft shadows
- Warm colour palette that feels approachable, not clinical

**What NOT to take:** Gamification, swipe mechanics, anything that reduces a professional to a card you dismiss.

### Linear (linear.app)
**What to take:** Keyboard-first speed, clean typography, restrained animation that adds polish without slowing you down, dark mode done right.

**Specifically:**
- Minimal UI chrome — the content is the interface
- Subtle hover states and transitions
- Dark mode feels native, not an afterthought
- Typography does the heavy lifting (weight, size, colour hierarchy)

**What NOT to take:** The density. Linear is for power users. YachtieLink is for people who might not be tech-savvy.

### Airbnb
**What to take:** Photo quality matters, the listing page structure (hero photo → details → reviews → action), trust through real content not badges.

**Specifically:**
- Photo gallery as the first thing you see
- Trust signals integrated naturally (reviews = endorsements, superhost = verified yachts)
- Clean, generous spacing
- Mobile-first but great on desktop

## Anti-Inspirations (What to Avoid)

### LinkedIn
**What NOT to do:** Dense corporate form feel, "profile completion" percentage that shames, skill endorsements from strangers, the feed taking over the product, dark patterns around premium.

YachtieLink profiles should feel personal and alive. LinkedIn profiles feel like HR paperwork.

### Yacht Crew Apps (CrewFO, Yachtie.com, DockWalk)
**What NOT to do:** Old-school job board aesthetic, cluttered forms, maritime clip art (anchors, ropes, compass roses), "yacht club" navy-and-gold colour scheme.

The 25% maritime feel in YachtieLink comes through colour naming (teal = deep ocean, sand = warm teak, coral = sunset over water) and Salty the mascot. Never through visual clichés.

### Generic Social Networks
**What NOT to do:** Feeds, like counts, follower metrics, public engagement numbers, algorithmic sorting, "people you may know." YachtieLink is a profile-first product. You get there via a direct link, not by scrolling a feed.

## Calibration Questions

When making a design choice, ask:

1. **Would this feel at home on Notion?** If it's too boring, add colour or personality.
2. **Would this feel at home on Bumble?** If it's too playful, add structure.
3. **Does this feel like LinkedIn?** If yes, it's too corporate. Warm it up.
4. **Does this feel like a dating app?** If yes, it's too casual. Add professionalism.
5. **Is the maritime feel in the content/colour, or in the decoration?** If decoration (anchors, waves), remove it.
